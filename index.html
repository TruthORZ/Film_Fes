EVERYONE IS A UNIVERSE

"We look to the stars, only to find ourselves."

ABOUT

For centuries, humanity has looked beyond the Earth, searching for signs of life in the vast unknown. But what if this search has never truly been about “them”?

From distant planets to incomprehensible signals, science fiction cinema repeatedly returns to a central question: what do we see when we encounter the alien?

Across five films, we invite you to look into the unknown—not to find answers, but to encounter reflections of memory, longing, belief, and the fragile limits of what it means to be human.

TRAILER

SELECTIONS

Solaris (1972)

Dir. Andrei Tarkovsky

A psychologist is sent to a space station orbiting the mysterious planet Solaris, where crew members begin encountering physical manifestations of their memories. As the boundary between reality and memory dissolves, the mission becomes increasingly difficult to bear. Here, the encounter with an alien intelligence unfolds as a confrontation with the past, where what appears external is deeply personal.

Contact (1997)

Dir. Robert Zemeckis

A scientist dedicated to the search for extraterrestrial intelligence receives a signal that appears to originate from an alien civilization. As she works to interpret it, questions of evidence, belief, and trust come to the forefront. The film explores how the desire to reach beyond Earth is shaped not only by scientific curiosity, but also by deeply held convictions about truth and existence.

Arrival (2016)

Dir. Denis Villeneuve

When mysterious spacecraft appear around the world, a linguist is recruited to communicate with their inhabitants. As she begins to understand their language, her perception of time and experience gradually shifts. In this process of communication, the unknown becomes a way of rethinking memory, choice, and what it means to understand one’s own life.

Ad Astra (2019)

Dir. James Gray

An astronaut travels across the solar system in search of his missing father, whose work may be linked to a series of unexplained cosmic events[cite: 37]. The journey takes him further from Earth, but also deeper into his own emotional isolation. As the distance from home grows, the film reflects on connection, absence, and the human need for meaning beyond the vastness of space.

Journey to the West (2023)

Dir. Kong Dashan

A small magazine devoted to extraterrestrial research sets out on a journey in search of alien life. What begins as a fanatical investigation gradually turns into something more uncertain, shaped by coincidence, belief, and miracle. In its quiet and often humorous way, the film suggests that the mystery of the universe is the mystery of life, and the answer to the universe is the answer to life.
