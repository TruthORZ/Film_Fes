EVERYONE IS A UNIVERSE

"We look to the stars, only to find ourselves."

ABOUT

For centuries, humanity has looked beyond the Earth, searching for signs of life in the vast unknown.

But what if this search has never truly been about “them”?

What are we really looking for when we look for others?

We begin with questions, signals, and the hope that the universe can be understood.

Between science and belief, the search becomes not only about discovery, but about what we choose to trust.

But when the unknown answers us, do we follow evidence—or belief?

The further we travel into space, the more we are confronted with distance—
not only between worlds, but between people.

In the silence of the cosmos, the search turns inward.

And in that silence, what are we truly trying to reach?

To encounter the unknown is to learn a new way of seeing.

As language shifts, so does our understanding of time, connection, and the meaning of our own lives.

If we begin to see differently, are we still the same person?

At the edge of survival, the universe becomes a question of meaning.

Beyond science and distance, it is human connection—memory, love—that gives the journey its purpose.

But if meaning comes from us, was the answer ever out there at all?

SELECTIONS

Journey to the West (2023)

Dir. Kong Dashan

A small magazine devoted to extraterrestrial research sets out on a journey in search of alien life. What begins as a fanatical investigation gradually turns into something more uncertain, shaped by coincidence, belief, and miracle. In its quiet and often humorous way, the film suggests that the mystery of the universe is the mystery of life, and the answer to the universe is the answer to life.

Contact (1997)

Dir. Robert Zemeckis

A scientist dedicated to the search for extraterrestrial intelligence receives a signal that appears to originate from an alien civilization. As she works to interpret it, questions of evidence, belief, and trust come to the forefront. The film explores how the desire to reach beyond Earth is shaped not only by scientific curiosity, but also by deeply held convictions about truth and existence.

Ad Astra (2019)

Dir. James Gray

An astronaut travels across the solar system in search of his missing father, whose work may be linked to a series of unexplained cosmic events. The journey takes him further from Earth, but also deeper into his own emotional isolation. As the distance from home grows, the film reflects on connection, absence, and the human need for meaning beyond the vastness of space.

Arrival (2016)

Dir. Denis Villeneuve

When mysterious spacecraft appear around the world, a linguist is recruited to communicate with their inhabitants. As she begins to understand their language, her perception of time and experience gradually shifts. In this process of communication, the unknown becomes a way of rethinking memory, choice, and what it means to understand one’s own life.

Interstellar (2014)

Dir. Christopher Nolan

In a near-future Earth facing environmental collapse, a team of astronauts sets out through a wormhole in search of a new home for humanity. As the mission unfolds across distant planets and altered perceptions of time, the journey becomes both a scientific and deeply personal undertaking. The film presents space exploration as a search for survival and meaning, where the vastness of the universe is shaped by human connection and the desire to transcend limits.
